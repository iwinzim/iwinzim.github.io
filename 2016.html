2016

SLICE FOR a SMILE

On a weekly basis (Thursday mornings 9am), we go to Harare Childrens Hospital and feed approximately +/- 150 children in the wards and the OI (Opportunistic Infection) Outpatients Clinic. The OI Outpatients Clinic are children suffering with AIDS who come on a weekly basis to get their medication and checkups with the doctors. Each child is given a meal pack consisting of a sandwich, banana and Mahewu.

This program ran from the months of January 2016 to November 2016 and will recommence in January 2017.

FRAIL CARE

On a monthly basis we distribute a $40.00 basic food hamper and $10.00 in cash. We have identified 103 destitute families (elderly and single mothers) who have no source of income. Our hamper includes the following items:

2kg Salt
375 ml Peanut Butter
2 kg Sugar
2 litre Oil
10 kg Mealie Meal
500g Kapenta
500g Sugar Beans
Tube Toothpaste
1kg Creamer
Bath Soap
1.6kg Soya
Candles
Boom Bar (Green Soap)
$10 cash

HARARE CHILDRENS HOSPITAL EMERGENCY ASSISTANCE

Mavies Chiwara is the Government Teacher based at the hospital. We work closely together and assist with needy cases. The following are some of the children we have helped:

1. Leonard Chigwede - 1 pint of blood due to severe burns
2. Vimbai Ruza Pain killers and 1 set of crutches after a cancerous growth led to
her leg being amputated.

iwin/ONE NATION UK CHARITY ORGANISATION

In April 2016, a charity organisation from the UK called One Nation UK sponsored:

50 x food hampers to child headed families, the destitute and elderly Hatcliffe Extension Residents to the sum of $1,350.00.

One Nation UK donated $155.00. From this $50.00 was used for the Slice for Smile Thursday morning program. Approximately 150 children were fed bananas, Mahewu and sandwiches.

BOREHOLE CHINHOYI GOVERNMENT PRIMARY SCHOOL

iWin received an anonymous donation for a borehole which was then sunk and installed at the Chinhoyi Government Primary School. This school has been experiencing water problems for a long time and was a welcome relief to all.

A ceremony on Tuesday 24th May 2016 was held at the school for the official handover of the borehole that had been drilled. The Minister of Public Service, Labour and Social Welfare the Honorable Mrs Mupfumira attended the event.

RESETTLEMENT OF 237 DISPLACED FAMILIES TO HATCLIFFE

There were 237 displaced families that iWin has been aiding and assisting in their resettlement to Hatcliffe. All the monies received towards relieving the plight of these destitute families were kindly donated by the general public.

The following were distributed by iWin with funds raised:

Borehole this was drilled to the value of $1,950.00. The official handover was done on the 1st June 2016.
Hand Pump A hand pump for the borehole was needed. An old pump from the Hatcliffe Main Masjid was utilized for this project.
Plastic Sheeting 3,100 meters of plastic sheeting was purchased and distributed. 166 families received 12 meters each and 71 families received 15 meters each. Plastic sheeting was provided for makeshift toilets as well.
Mealie Meal 230 x 10kg bags of Mealie Meal were distributed.
Mahewu 240 sachets of Mahewu were distributed to the children.
Sugar 300 x 2kg packets of sugar were distributed.
Bottled Water 10 cases x 12 bottles were distributed.
Nik Nacks 300 packets were distributed.
Blankets 200 x baby blankets and 45 x double size were distributed.
Seeds 10 packets of various vegetable seeds were distributed.
Plastic Buckets with lids 25 x 20 litre buckets were distributed.
Second hand clothing that had also been donated was distributed.

DISTRIBUTION OF HAMPERS IN MELFORD

A very generous donor approached iWin in June 2016 to distribute hampers with a total value of $5,000.00 to the needy.

There were 160 hampers with the approximate value of $30.00. With their assistance as well, hampers were given to needy families in Melford (40km beyond Ruwa).

iWin AND IWA (INDIAN WOMANS ASSOCIATION)

iWin approached the Indian Womans Association (IWA) in June 2016 to see if they could help assist the displaced in Hatcliffe.

IWA was very happy to help and donated goods to the value of $4,000.00.

On the 14th June 2016, IWA went to Hatcliffe Settlement with iWin to distribute the goods they had generously donated.
